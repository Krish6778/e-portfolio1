Top 50 Learnings
Key takeaways from the Employability & Skill Enhancement Lab
A. Communication Skills
  • Communicate clearly and briefly to avoid confusion.
  • Building confidence by maintaining steady eye contact.
  • Listening is as crucial as expressing your thoughts.
  • Body language and tone amplify your spoken message.
  • Group discussions build speaking skills and flexibility.
  • Reducing fillers (“like”, “um”) brings fluency.
  • Practicing public talks eliminates stage fright.
  • Accurate pronunciation and clear enunciation matter.
  • Written work should be organized and error-free.
  • Confident gestures and posture speak volumes without words.
B. Teamwork & Collaboration
  • Teamwork enhances patience and adaptability.
  • Lead by inspiring and supporting, not commanding.
  • Diverse viewpoints foster original solutions.
  • Resolving disagreements keeps teams productive.
  • Delegating work increases group efficiency.
  • Encouragement moves the team forward faster.
  • Give feedback kindly and constructively.
  • Adjust to different working habits and personalities.
  • Share duties for a fair workload.
  • Solving problems together forges strong teams.
C. Critical Thinking
  • Analyze situations before acting for better results.
  • Brainstorming stirs up creative solutions.
  • Find root causes before proposing fixes.
  • Base your arguments on data, not guesswork.
  • Weigh the pros and cons before deciding.
  • Stay curious—keep asking questions!
  • Practice with real-life scenarios for adaptability.
  • Manage your time to boost productivity.
  • Keep calm in pressure situations to think clearly.
  • Set clear objectives and track your progress.
D. Technical & Digital Skills
  • Create engaging presentations in PowerPoint.
  • Craft polished resumes using MS Word or Canva.
  • Master basic Excel tools for organizing data.
  • Send professional emails with proper etiquette.
  • Use the internet to research efficiently and ethically.
  • Collaborate online with tools like Google Meet or Teams.
  • Keep your online accounts and devices secure.
  • Host virtual meetings with professionalism.
  • Build your professional network on LinkedIn.
  • Be aware of your digital “footprint” and online presence.
E. Etiquette & Personality
  • Dress for the occasion and the workplace culture.
  • Being on time shows commitment and respect.
  • Confidence—without arrogance—draws opportunities your way.
  • Maintaining discipline helps achieve consistency.
  • Positive energy is attractive and employable.
  • Manage workplace emotions and relationships wisely.
  • Continuously update and hone your skills.
  • Your ethics and conduct reflect your personal brand.
  • Welcome criticism as a tool for growth.
  • Adopt a growth mindset for a lasting career journey.
